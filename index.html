Daily Social Posts

Turning 65 soon? Your Medicare enrollment window is only 7 months long. Many people miss it and end up with penalties. If you're confused about when to enroll, feel free to message me.

Medicare does NOT cover dental, vision, or hearing. Many retirees are surprised by that. Planning for those costs ahead of time can make retirement much easier.

One of the biggest Medicare mistakes people make is assuming their spouse’s coverage will continue forever. If you are approaching retirement, it’s important to understand your options.

I recently helped someone turning 65 who didn't realize their enrollment window had already started. Luckily we caught it early and avoided penalties.

Retirement should be about enjoying life, not worrying about unexpected medical bills. Planning ahead can make a huge difference.

Many people think life insurance is only for young families. In reality many retirees use life insurance to cover final expenses or leave something behind for loved ones.

Quick question: What confuses you most about Medicare?
